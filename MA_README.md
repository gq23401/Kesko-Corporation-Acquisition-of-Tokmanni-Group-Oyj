# Kesko Corporation — Acquisition of Tokmanni Group Oyj

## M&A Accretion / Dilution Analysis

A comprehensive merger analysis model for Kesko Corporation's (KESKOB.HE) hypothetical acquisition of Tokmanni Group Oyj (TOKMAN.HE), built following CFI Financial Modeling Best Practices and Financial Modeling Guidelines.

**Recommendation: PROCEED WITH CAUTION — Dilutive without synergies; accretive with realisable synergies above EUR 26M**

### Strategic Rationale

Kesko's acquisition of Tokmanni would consolidate Finland's two largest non-grocery retail presences, creating a EUR 14.2B revenue Nordic retail group operating across grocery, discount, building, and automotive segments. Key strategic drivers include procurement synergies from combined purchasing power, logistics consolidation through the recently completed Hyvinkaa center, K-Plussa loyalty programme cross-selling into Tokmanni's 200+ store network, and private label expansion opportunities.

## Accretion / Dilution Results

| Metric | Without Synergies | With Synergies |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| Kesko Standalone EPS | EUR 1.09 | EUR 1.09 |
| Pro Forma EPS | EUR 1.04 | EUR 1.12 |
| EPS Change | (EUR 0.05) | +EUR 0.04 |
| **Accretion / (Dilution)** | **(4.6%) DILUTIVE** | **+3.5% ACCRETIVE** |
| Break-Even Pre-Tax Synergies | **EUR 25.7M** | — |

### Key Finding

The transaction is **dilutive by 4.6%** on a standalone basis due to the high acquisition debt cost and the PPA amortization charge. However, with the EUR 85M of identified run-rate synergies (EUR 45M cost + EUR 40M revenue), the deal becomes **accretive by 3.5%**. The break-even requires only EUR 25.7M in annual pre-tax synergies — well below the EUR 85M identified, providing a **3.3x synergy cushion**.

Pre-deal leverage is investment-grade at 0.8x. Post-deal leverage increases to 1.4x — still manageable but a meaningful step-up. Rating agencies would likely place Kesko on negative outlook pending integration execution. Deleveraging to sub-1.0x would require approximately 2-3 years of free cash flow allocation.

| EV / EBITDA | 8.1x |
| EV / EBIT | 18.6x |
| P / E (Offer Price) | 19.2x |

The 8.1x EV/EBITDA represents a premium to Tokmanni's recent trading range (~6-7x) but is in line with precedent Finnish retail transactions. The premium is justified by the strategic control premium, synergy value (EUR 85M run-rate ÷ 10% discount = EUR 850M NPV), and Tokmanni's depressed current valuation driven by Dollarstore integration challenges.
